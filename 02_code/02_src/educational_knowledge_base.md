# SECTION 1: Academic Policies

---

**Chunk ID:** AP-001
**Title:** Attendance Policy — General Requirements
**Content:** Students at Avendale Academic Institute (AAI) are expected to attend all scheduled class sessions for each enrolled course. Regular attendance is considered essential to academic success and is monitored by instructors throughout the semester. A student who accumulates absences exceeding 20% of the total scheduled class meetings in any course may be subject to grade penalties, including a failing grade at the instructor's discretion. Attendance records are maintained by the instructor and may be submitted to the Office of Academic Affairs upon request. Students are responsible for notifying their instructor in advance of any anticipated absence. Excused absences may be granted for documented medical emergencies, bereavement, or officially recognized institutional activities. Unexcused absences do not exempt students from completing assigned coursework.

---

**Chunk ID:** AP-002
**Title:** Attendance Policy — Excused Absence Procedures
**Content:** To request an excused absence at AAI, students must submit a written request to their instructor within three (3) business days of the missed session, accompanied by appropriate documentation. Accepted documentation includes a signed medical certificate from a licensed healthcare provider, an official bereavement notice, or a letter from the relevant AAI department for institutional activities. Instructors review documentation and notify students of approval or denial within five (5) business days. Approved excused absences do not count toward the 20% attendance threshold. Students granted excused absences are entitled to complete missed assessments within a timeframe agreed upon with the instructor. Falsification of absence documentation constitutes academic misconduct and is subject to disciplinary action under the Academic Integrity Policy.

---

**Chunk ID:** AP-003
**Title:** Course Registration — Overview and Eligibility
**Content:** Course registration at AAI is managed through the Student Academic Portal (SAP). Eligible students may register during their designated registration window, which is assigned based on academic standing and credit hours completed. Students with senior standing (90+ credit hours) register first, followed by juniors, sophomores, and freshmen. Students must have no outstanding financial holds, academic holds, or administrative flags on their accounts to access registration. Registration windows open approximately six (6) weeks before the start of each semester. Students are encouraged to consult with their academic advisor before finalizing their course selections to ensure alignment with degree requirements. Registration is not complete until all tuition and fee obligations for the upcoming semester are addressed.

---

**Chunk ID:** AP-004
**Title:** Course Enrollment — Maximum and Minimum Credit Hours
**Content:** Full-time enrollment at AAI requires a minimum of 12 credit hours per semester. Part-time enrollment is defined as fewer than 12 credit hours. The standard full-time load is 15–18 credit hours per semester. Students who wish to enroll in more than 18 credit hours must obtain written approval from their academic advisor and the Dean of their college. Overload requests are evaluated based on the student's cumulative GPA (minimum 3.0 required), prior semester performance, and demonstrated academic readiness. Enrollment below 12 credit hours may affect financial aid eligibility. Students are advised to review their financial aid status with the Office of Financial Services before reducing their credit load below full-time status.

---

**Chunk ID:** AP-005
**Title:** Add/Drop Period — Rules and Deadlines
**Content:** The Add/Drop period at AAI occurs during the first ten (10) calendar days of each semester. During this period, students may add open courses, drop courses without academic penalty, or swap sections without fee. After the Add/Drop deadline, no new course additions are permitted. Courses dropped during the Add/Drop period do not appear on the student's official transcript. Students must use the Student Academic Portal (SAP) to process Add/Drop requests. Instructor signatures are not required during the Add/Drop window unless the course is restricted. Students are responsible for ensuring that dropped courses do not reduce their enrollment below minimum requirements, which could affect financial aid, housing, or visa status for international students.

---

**Chunk ID:** AP-006
**Title:** Course Withdrawal — Policy and Procedures
**Content:** After the Add/Drop period closes, students may formally withdraw from a course through the end of Week 10 of a standard 16-week semester. A course withdrawal results in a grade of "W" on the student's transcript, which does not affect GPA calculation but is a permanent record. To withdraw, students must submit a Course Withdrawal Form through the Student Academic Portal (SAP) and obtain approval from their academic advisor. Withdrawal after Week 10 requires additional approval from the Dean of the relevant college and is granted only in cases of documented extenuating circumstances. Excessive withdrawals (more than three "W" grades total) may trigger an academic review. Students should consult the Office of Financial Services before withdrawing, as it may affect financial aid status.

---

**Chunk ID:** AP-007
**Title:** GPA Calculation — Standard Method
**Content:** Grade Point Average (GPA) at AAI is calculated by dividing the total quality points earned by the total credit hours attempted. Quality points are determined by multiplying the credit hours of a course by the grade point value assigned to the earned grade. The grading scale is as follows: A = 4.0, A- = 3.7, B+ = 3.3, B = 3.0, B- = 2.7, C+ = 2.3, C = 2.0, C- = 1.7, D+ = 1.3, D = 1.0, F = 0.0. Grades of W (Withdrawal), I (Incomplete), P (Pass), and S (Satisfactory) are not included in GPA calculations. Cumulative GPA reflects all graded coursework taken at AAI. Transfer credits are recorded on the transcript but are not included in the AAI cumulative GPA calculation.

**Chunk ID:** AP-008
**Title:** Academic Standing — Good Standing Requirements
**Content:** A student at AAI is considered to be in Good Academic Standing when their cumulative GPA meets or exceeds 2.0 and they have successfully completed at least 67% of all credit hours attempted (Satisfactory Academic Progress). Good Standing students have full access to registration, academic services, student activities, and financial aid eligibility, subject to individual program requirements. Academic standing is evaluated at the end of each fall and spring semester. Students who fall below the Good Standing threshold at the end of a semester will be placed on Academic Probation. Maintaining Good Standing is a prerequisite for participation in honors programs, competitive internships facilitated through AAI, and study abroad opportunities.

---

**Chunk ID:** AP-009
**Title:** Academic Probation — Conditions and Requirements
**Content:** A student is placed on Academic Probation at AAI when their cumulative GPA falls below 2.0 or their completion rate drops below 67% of attempted credit hours. Probation status is recorded on the student's internal academic record and communicated via official written notice from the Office of Academic Affairs. Students on probation must enroll in no more than 13 credit hours per semester and are required to meet with their academic advisor at least once every four weeks. Students on probation must also complete an Academic Success Plan (ASP) outlining specific improvement goals. Probation status is reviewed at the end of the subsequent semester. If requirements are met and GPA rises above 2.0, the student is returned to Good Standing.

---

**Chunk ID:** AP-010
**Title:** Academic Suspension — Policy and Duration
**Content:** Academic Suspension at AAI is imposed when a student on Academic Probation fails to achieve a cumulative GPA of 2.0 or higher by the end of the probationary semester, or when a student's GPA falls to 1.0 or below at any point. Suspension requires the student to sit out for one full academic semester (excluding summer sessions). During suspension, students may not enroll in courses, access campus academic facilities as enrolled students, or receive institutional financial aid. Students wishing to return after suspension must apply for reinstatement through the Office of Academic Affairs, submit a written academic plan, and receive approval from the Dean of their college. Reinstatement is not automatic and is reviewed on a case-by-case basis.

---

**Chunk ID:** AP-011
**Title:** Academic Dismissal — Conditions and Policy
**Content:** Academic Dismissal at AAI occurs when a student fails to meet reinstatement conditions following suspension, or when a student is suspended a second time within their academic career at AAI. Dismissal is a permanent separation from the institution and is distinct from suspension. A dismissed student may petition for readmission after a minimum of two full academic years have elapsed. Readmission petitions must be submitted to the Academic Appeals Committee, include a written personal statement, evidence of personal or professional development during the absence, and letters of support. The Academic Appeals Committee reviews petitions and issues decisions within 30 business days. Readmission is not guaranteed.

**Chunk ID:** AP-012
**Title:** Degree Requirements — General Framework
**Content:** All undergraduate degree programs at AAI require the completion of a minimum of 120 credit hours, including General Education Requirements (GERs), major-specific requirements, and elective coursework. General Education Requirements comprise a core curriculum covering written communication, quantitative reasoning, natural sciences, social sciences, humanities, and ethical reasoning, totaling 42 credit hours. Major requirements vary by program and are outlined in the official Program of Study for each degree. Students must maintain a minimum cumulative GPA of 2.0 and a minimum GPA of 2.0 in their major coursework to qualify for graduation. Specific programs may require higher GPAs; students should consult their program handbook for details.

**Chunk ID:** AP-013
**Title:** Graduation — Application Requirements
**Content:** Students intending to graduate from AAI must submit a formal Graduation Application through the Student Academic Portal (SAP) no later than the end of Week 4 of their final semester. Applications submitted after this deadline may result in graduation being deferred to the following semester. The Graduation Application triggers a Degree Audit conducted by the Registrar's Office to verify that all degree requirements have been met. Students are notified of audit results within 15 business days. Unmet requirements identified in the audit must be addressed before graduation clearance is issued. Students are encouraged to complete an informal degree audit with their academic advisor at least one semester prior to applying.

**Chunk ID:** AP-014
**Title:** Transfer Credit — Acceptance Policy
**Content:** AAI accepts transfer credits from regionally accredited institutions, subject to evaluation by the Registrar's Office and the relevant academic department. Transfer credits are evaluated for equivalency to AAI courses. Courses with a grade of C or higher are eligible for transfer consideration. Transfer credits do not affect the AAI cumulative GPA. A maximum of 60 credit hours may be transferred toward an undergraduate degree. At least 30 of the final 45 credit hours required for graduation must be completed at AAI (residency requirement). Transfer students must submit official transcripts from all prior institutions as part of the admissions process. Advanced Placement (AP) and International Baccalaureate (IB) credits are evaluated separately.

---

**Chunk ID:** AP-015
**Title:** Transfer Credit — AP and IB Credit Evaluation
**Content:** AAI recognizes Advanced Placement (AP) and International Baccalaureate (IB) examination scores for potential course credit. AP exams with a score of 4 or 5 may be awarded equivalent course credit as determined by the relevant academic department. IB Higher Level exams with a score of 5 or above may be granted equivalent course credit. Standard Level IB exams are evaluated on a case-by-case basis. Students must submit official College Board AP score reports or IB transcripts to the Registrar's Office for evaluation. Credit awards are not automatic and depend on program relevance. AP/IB credits are counted toward the maximum 60 transfer credit hours. Awarded credits appear on the AAI transcript with a notation indicating the source.

**Chunk ID:** AP-016
**Title:** Double Major — Policy and Requirements
**Content:** Students at AAI may pursue a double major, provided they meet the requirements of both programs simultaneously. A formal Declaration of Double Major must be filed with the Registrar's Office and approved by advisors in both departments. Students must maintain a minimum cumulative GPA of 2.5 to declare a double major. Both majors must be completed and all requirements satisfied for a double major to appear on the diploma and transcript. There is no additional degree conferred; both majors are recognized on a single bachelor's degree. Students pursuing a double major may need to exceed the standard 120 credit hour minimum. No coursework may simultaneously satisfy the core requirements of both majors unless explicitly permitted by both program handbooks.

**Chunk ID:** AP-017
**Title:** Minor — Declaration and Requirements
**Content:** A minor at AAI consists of a defined set of courses in a secondary field of study, typically requiring 18–21 credit hours. Students may declare a minor beginning in their sophomore year, provided they have completed any listed prerequisites. A Minor Declaration Form must be submitted to the Registrar's Office. Minors must be completed by graduation and will appear on the official transcript. A student may complete more than one minor. Minor coursework may overlap with major requirements only up to 6 credit hours, unless a specific exception is granted by the relevant department chairs. Minors are not available as standalone programs; they must be pursued alongside a bachelor's degree program.

**Chunk ID:** AP-018
**Title:** Credit Hours — Definition and Classification
**Content:** At AAI, one credit hour represents one hour of direct instruction and a minimum of two hours of student preparation per week over a 16-week semester, or equivalent academic engagement for alternative formats. Laboratory courses typically award 1 credit hour per 3 hours of scheduled lab time per week. Online and hybrid courses follow equivalent workload standards. Credit hours are assigned to courses based on instructional contact time and expected student effort. The minimum credit hour value for any course is 1. Independent study and research credits follow a similar formula. Credit hour values are listed in the official Course Catalog and may not be modified by instructors. Students should confirm credit hour values before registration.

**Chunk ID:** AP-019
**Title:** Semester Structure — Academic Calendar
**Content:** AAI operates on a standard two-semester academic year, consisting of a Fall Semester (16 weeks) and a Spring Semester (16 weeks), plus an optional Summer Session (8 weeks). The Fall Semester typically begins in late August and concludes in mid-December. The Spring Semester begins in mid-January and concludes in early May. The Summer Session is offered in two sub-sessions of four weeks each or one full eight-week block. Final examination periods are scheduled at the conclusion of the fall and spring semesters. The official Academic Calendar, including registration windows, holidays, and deadlines, is published on the AAI institutional website and the Student Academic Portal (SAP) at least six months in advance.

**Chunk ID:** AP-020
**Title:** Academic Appeals — Overview and Eligibility
**Content:** Students at AAI have the right to appeal academic decisions, including course grades, academic probation or suspension notices, and denial of academic accommodations. To be eligible, an appeal must be submitted within 15 business days of the official decision date. Appeals must be based on one or more of the following grounds: procedural error in the application of institutional policy, new material evidence not available at the time of the original decision, or demonstrated bias or prejudice in the decision-making process. Disagreement with a grade or policy outcome alone, without substantive grounds, is not a valid basis for appeal. Appeals are submitted through the Academic Appeals Portal on the SAP.

---

**Chunk ID:** AP-021
**Title:** Academic Appeals — Submission Process
**Content:** To submit an academic appeal at AAI, students must complete the Academic Appeal Form available on the Student Academic Portal (SAP). The form requires a clear statement of the decision being appealed, the specific grounds for the appeal, a narrative explanation, and supporting documentation. Supporting documentation may include medical records, official correspondence, graded materials, or other relevant evidence. Incomplete submissions are returned to the student with a request for additional information. Once a complete appeal is received, the Academic Appeals Committee has 20 business days to review the case and issue a decision. Students are notified of outcomes in writing. Decisions issued by the Academic Appeals Committee are considered final and are not subject to further internal appeal.

**Chunk ID:** AP-022
**Title:** Assessment Policies — General Standards
**Content:** All courses at AAI must include a documented assessment plan that is distributed to students in the course syllabus during the first week of class. Assessment plans must include a description of each graded component, its weight in the final grade, the submission method, and the deadline. Instructors are responsible for providing grading rubrics or criteria for all major assessments worth 15% or more of the final grade. Assessment plans may not be substantially changed after the Add/Drop period closes without written consent from the majority of enrolled students and approval from the department chair. Assessments are designed to evaluate student achievement of defined learning outcomes for each course.

**Chunk ID:** AP-023
**Title:** Assessment Policies — Grading and Feedback
**Content:** Instructors at AAI are required to return graded work to students within 14 calendar days of the submission deadline for assignments and within 7 calendar days following the administration of examinations. Final grades must be submitted to the Registrar's Office no later than 72 hours after the scheduled final examination for each course. Grades submitted after this deadline may delay degree audits and graduation processing. Students have the right to receive written feedback on all major assessments. If a student believes a grade is incorrect, they should first attempt to resolve the matter directly with the instructor before initiating a formal grade appeal. Grade appeals are governed by the Academic Appeals Policy.

**Chunk ID:** AP-024
**Title:** Learning Outcomes — Institutional Requirements
**Content:** Every course offered at AAI must have clearly defined Student Learning Outcomes (SLOs) that are published in the course syllabus and aligned with the broader program-level and institutional-level learning goals. SLOs describe the knowledge, skills, and competencies students are expected to demonstrate upon successful completion of the course. Assessment tools used in the course must be aligned with the stated SLOs. Faculty are required to report on SLO attainment data as part of the annual program review process. The Office of Academic Affairs coordinates the assessment of institutional learning outcomes and uses aggregated data to inform curriculum development, resource allocation, and accreditation reporting.

**Chunk ID:** AP-025
**Title:** Student Responsibilities — Academic Engagement
**Content:** Students enrolled at AAI bear primary responsibility for their own academic success. This includes attending classes regularly, completing all assigned coursework by stated deadlines, actively participating in class discussions and activities, and seeking assistance when needed. Students are expected to read and adhere to all course syllabi, institutional policies, and program requirements. Students must maintain familiarity with the Academic Calendar, registration deadlines, and graduation requirements. Proactive communication with instructors and advisors is expected, particularly when facing challenges that may affect academic performance. AAI provides a range of support services to assist students, but utilization of those services is the student's responsibility.

**Chunk ID:** AP-026
**Title:** Incomplete Grade — Policy and Resolution
**Content:** A grade of Incomplete (I) may be assigned at AAI when a student has completed at least 70% of required coursework but is unable to finish the remaining work due to documented extenuating circumstances beyond their control, such as a medical emergency or family crisis. The instructor and student must agree to an Incomplete Grade Contract, specifying the remaining work and a completion deadline, which may not exceed 8 weeks from the last day of the semester. If the student does not complete the outstanding work by the agreed deadline, the I grade is automatically converted to an F. Extensions to Incomplete contracts are not granted without documented justification and written approval from the department chair.

**Chunk ID:** AP-027
**Title:** Grade of Pass/Fail — Elective Courses
**Content:** AAI permits students to elect Pass/Fail grading for certain elective courses, subject to specific conditions. A student may designate up to 12 total credit hours during their academic career as Pass/Fail. Courses designated as Pass/Fail must be approved electives outside the student's major, minor, or General Education Requirements. The Pass/Fail election must be declared no later than the end of the Add/Drop period. A grade of Pass (P) is awarded for final course grades of C or above and does not affect GPA. A grade of Fail (F) is recorded and does count in GPA calculation. Students may not retroactively convert a letter grade to Pass/Fail or vice versa after the Add/Drop deadline.

**Chunk ID:** AP-028
**Title:** Auditing a Course — Policy
**Content:** Students may audit a course at AAI, meaning they may attend class sessions without receiving academic credit or a grade. Auditors are not required to submit assignments or take examinations unless the instructor requires it as a condition of participation. Audited courses appear on the transcript with an AU notation. Tuition for audited courses is charged at the standard per-credit rate. Audit status must be declared at registration or during the Add/Drop period and may not be changed to credit-bearing status after the Add/Drop deadline. Auditing students are expected to observe all classroom conduct policies. Instructors have discretion to limit the number of auditing students in their class.

**Chunk ID:** AP-029
**Title:** Academic Renewal — Policy for Returning Students
**Content:** Students who have been away from AAI for a minimum of five consecutive years may apply for Academic Renewal upon reinstatement. Under Academic Renewal, coursework completed more than five years prior with grades below C may be excluded from the cumulative GPA calculation, provided those courses are not required for the student's current degree program. Students may apply for Academic Renewal only once during their academic career at AAI. The application is submitted to the Office of Academic Affairs with supporting documentation. Courses excluded under Academic Renewal remain on the official transcript with a notation. Academic Renewal does not affect the calculation of Satisfactory Academic Progress for financial aid purposes.

**Chunk ID:** AP-030
**Title:** Course Repeat Policy
**Content:** Students at AAI may repeat a course in which they received a grade of C- or below, including F. When a course is repeated, only the most recent grade is used in the cumulative GPA calculation, though both attempts remain on the transcript. A course may be repeated a maximum of two times (three total attempts). A third repeat requires written approval from the Dean of the relevant college and is granted only in exceptional circumstances. Repeated courses are flagged on the transcript with an "R" notation on the original attempt. Repeating a course does not automatically replace the original grade retroactively for any previous GPA calculations, honors designations, or financial aid assessments.

**Chunk ID:** AP-031
**Title:** Prerequisites — Enforcement Policy
**Content:** Prerequisites listed in the AAI Course Catalog are strictly enforced. Students may not enroll in a course without satisfying all listed prerequisites, unless a formal prerequisite waiver is granted. Prerequisite waivers must be requested through the Student Academic Portal (SAP) and require written approval from the instructor of record and the department chair. Waivers are granted in cases where the student can demonstrate equivalent preparation through prior coursework, professional experience, or examination. Students who enroll in a course without meeting prerequisites and without an approved waiver may be administratively dropped from the course without refund. It is the student's responsibility to verify prerequisites before registering.

**Chunk ID:** AP-032
**Title:** Independent Study — Policy and Registration
**Content:** Independent Study at AAI allows students to pursue individualized academic projects under the supervision of a faculty mentor. Independent Study credits range from 1 to 3 credit hours per semester. To register, students must identify a willing faculty supervisor, agree on a written Independent Study Proposal outlining objectives, deliverables, meeting schedule, and assessment criteria, and obtain approval from the department chair. The proposal must be submitted to the Registrar's Office no later than the end of the Add/Drop period. Students may earn a maximum of 9 independent study credit hours toward their degree. Grading follows standard letter grade conventions. Independent Study may not substitute for required core or major courses without explicit departmental approval.

**Chunk ID:** AP-033

**Content:** Students who participate in AAI-approved study abroad programs may earn academic credit for coursework completed at the host institution. Students must submit a Course Approval Form for each course they wish to transfer for credit. Upon return, official transcripts from the host institution must be submitted to the Registrar's Office. Credits are evaluated for equivalency according to the same standards as domestic transfer credit. Credits earned abroad are subject to the standard 60-hour transfer credit maximum. **Chunk ID:** AP-034

Eligibility for the Honors Program requires a cumulative GPA of 3.5 or above at time of application and completion of at least 30 credit hours at AAI. Students who do not maintain program requirements may be dismissed from the Honors Program by the Director of Honors.

**Chunk ID:** AP-035
**Title:** Dean's List — Policy
**Content:** The Dean's List at AAI is a recognition of outstanding academic achievement awarded at the conclusion of each fall and spring semester. To qualify, a student must have completed a minimum of 12 credit hours in the relevant semester with a semester GPA of 3.7 or above. Courses graded as Pass/Fail are not counted toward the 12-credit hour minimum. Students on Academic Probation at any point during the semester are not eligible. Dean's List recognition is noted on the student's official transcript. A formal letter of acknowledgment is sent to qualifying students by the Office of Academic Affairs. Students may be placed on the Dean's List multiple times throughout their academic career.

**Chunk ID:** AP-036

**Content:** AAI awards Latin Honors to graduating students who have achieved a high level of academic performance. Latin Honors designations are based on cumulative GPA at time of graduation. Cum Laude is awarded for a cumulative GPA of 3.5–3.64. Magna Cum Laude is awarded for a cumulative GPA of 3.65–3.84. Summa Cum Laude is awarded for a cumulative GPA of 3.85–4.0. Latin Honors designations are printed on the diploma and noted permanently on the official transcript. Students are notified of their honors designation during the graduation clearance process.

**Chunk ID:** AP-037

**Content:** AAI reserves the right to cancel any course due to insufficient enrollment, instructor unavailability, or other institutional reasons. Students enrolled in a cancelled course are notified at least 10 business days before the start of the semester when possible. In cases of cancellation, students are given priority access to register for alternative courses and receive full refunds of any fees paid for the cancelled course. If a course is cancelled after the semester has begun due to an emergency, the Office of Academic Affairs will communicate alternative arrangements, including reassignment to another section or rescheduling. Students are not penalized academically for course cancellations beyond their control.

**Chunk ID:** AP-038
**Title:** Satisfactory Academic Progress — Financial Aid Standard
**Content:** To maintain eligibility for institutional financial aid at AAI, students must meet Satisfactory Academic Progress (SAP) standards evaluated each semester. SAP requires students to: (1) maintain a minimum cumulative GPA of 2.0, (2) successfully complete at least 67% of all attempted credit hours, and (3) complete their degree program within 150% of the published minimum credit hours required. Students who fail to meet SAP standards receive a warning for their first semester of non-compliance. Financial aid is suspended if SAP is not restored in the following semester. Students may appeal SAP suspension by submitting documentation of mitigating circumstances to the Office of Financial Services.

**Chunk ID:** AP-039
**Title:** Probationary Enrollment — Restricted Registration
**Content:** Students on Academic Probation at AAI are subject to restricted enrollment, limiting them to a maximum of 13 credit hours per semester. This restriction is automatically enforced through the Student Academic Portal (SAP) and cannot be overridden without written approval from the Dean of the student's college. The enrollment restriction is intended to allow students to focus on improving academic performance without the risk of overextension. Students subject to this restriction are encouraged to select courses strategically in consultation with their academic advisor, prioritizing courses necessary for GPA recovery and degree progress. The credit hour restriction is lifted once the student returns to Good Academic Standing.

# SECTION 2: Academic Integrity & Responsible AI Use

---

**Chunk ID:** AI-001
**Title:** Academic Integrity — Core Principles
**Content:** Academic integrity is a foundational value at Avendale Academic Institute (AAI). All students are expected to uphold the principles of honesty, trust, fairness, respect, and responsibility in all academic work. Academic integrity means that submitted work represents the student's own original effort, that sources are properly acknowledged, and that academic processes are not subverted or manipulated. Violations of academic integrity undermine the educational mission of AAI and the value of credentials earned by all students. Every student is presumed to have read and understood the AAI Academic Integrity Policy upon enrollment. Ignorance of the policy is not accepted as a defense in misconduct proceedings.

---

**Chunk ID:** AI-002
**Title:** Plagiarism — Definition and Examples
**Content:** Plagiarism at AAI is defined as the use of another person's ideas, words, data, or creative work without proper attribution, whether intentional or unintentional. Forms of plagiarism include: copying text from a source without quotation marks or citation; paraphrasing someone else's ideas without attribution; submitting work previously produced for another course without instructor permission (self-plagiarism); using another student's work as one's own; and presenting AI-generated text as original personal work without disclosure. All submitted academic work must be original and properly documented. Plagiarism is treated as a serious academic offense regardless of the student's intent. Both direct and mosaic plagiarism (patch-writing) are subject to disciplinary action.

---

**Chunk ID:** AI-003
**Title:** Cheating — Definition and Scope
**Content:** Cheating at AAI refers to any attempt to gain an unfair academic advantage through dishonest means during an assessment or evaluation. Examples include: using unauthorized notes, devices, or materials during a closed-book examination; copying from another student's exam paper; receiving unauthorized assistance during an individually assessed task; accessing prohibited resources during an online assessment; and submitting purchased or externally produced work as one's own. Cheating also includes assisting another student in cheating, which is treated with equal seriousness. The scope of cheating includes all forms of assessed academic work, whether administered in person, online, or in hybrid formats. Both the student who cheats and any student who knowingly facilitates cheating may face penalties.

---

**Chunk ID:** AI-004
**Title:** Fabrication — Definition and Policy
**Content:** Fabrication is the invention or falsification of data, results, citations, or other information submitted for academic evaluation at AAI. Examples include: inventing experimental results or survey responses; falsifying laboratory data; citing sources that do not exist or do not contain the referenced information; misrepresenting research methodology; and altering or selectively omitting data to support a preferred conclusion. Fabrication is considered one of the most serious forms of academic misconduct because it undermines the integrity of scholarship and research. Instructors and supervisors are encouraged to verify citations and data in student submissions when warranted. Fabrication findings in research contexts may be referred to the Research Ethics Board in addition to the standard misconduct process.

---

**Chunk ID:** AI-005
**Title:** Unauthorized Collaboration — Policy
**Content:** Unauthorized collaboration at AAI occurs when students work together on an academic task that has been designated as individual work. Collaboration is unauthorized when it is not explicitly permitted by the instructor in the course syllabus or assignment instructions. Examples include: dividing individual assignment tasks among multiple students and compiling a joint submission; sharing answers to problem sets designated as individual work; and revising or editing another student's individual assignment in ways that substantively alter its content. Students should assume that all work is individual unless the instructor explicitly designates it as collaborative. Group projects formally designated as such are governed by separate Group Assessment policies. Communication about individual assignments should be limited to general discussion of concepts, not specific solutions.

---

**Chunk ID:** AI-006
**Title:** Citation Practices — Required Standards
**Content:** All academic work submitted at AAI that draws upon external sources must include proper citations following a recognized citation style. The required citation style for each course is specified in the course syllabus. Common citation styles used at AAI include APA (American Psychological Association), MLA (Modern Language Association), and Chicago/Turabian. Students are expected to cite all direct quotations, paraphrased ideas, data, images, and any work not entirely their own. In-text citations and a reference list or bibliography are required where applicable. Proper citation is not only an academic integrity requirement but also a professional practice skill. The AAI Writing Center provides citation guides and workshops to assist students.

---

**Chunk ID:** AI-007
**Title:** Research Ethics — Student Responsibilities
**Content:** Students engaged in research at AAI, whether for coursework, independent study, or collaborative projects, are expected to adhere to established research ethics standards. This includes obtaining informed consent from human research participants, ensuring participant confidentiality, avoiding harm, and conducting research with honesty and transparency. Research involving human participants must receive Institutional Review Board (IRB) approval before data collection begins. Research involving animals must receive approval from the Animal Care and Ethics Committee. Students must accurately represent their research methods, data, and findings. Misrepresentation of any aspect of the research process constitutes academic misconduct. Faculty supervisors are jointly responsible for ensuring ethical compliance in student research.

---

**Chunk ID:** AI-008
**Title:** Examination Integrity — Student Obligations
**Content:** During any examination at AAI, students are obligated to uphold the highest standards of academic integrity. Students must follow all instructions issued by the proctor or instructor. No communication with other examinees is permitted unless explicitly allowed. Only materials authorized by the instructor may be present at the examination station. Electronic devices not explicitly permitted by the instructor must be stored and inaccessible. Students must not access external resources, including the internet, during closed-book examinations. Students who observe potential misconduct during an examination should report it to the proctor. Any attempt to copy, transmit, or record examination questions or responses constitutes academic misconduct. Violation of examination integrity policies may result in immediate dismissal from the examination.

---

**Chunk ID:** AI-009
**Title:** Assignment Integrity — Submission Standards
**Content:** Students at AAI must submit their own original work for all academic assignments unless collaboration is explicitly authorized. Submissions must reflect the student's individual intellectual effort and must comply with all assignment instructions. Submitting work completed by another person, in whole or in part, constitutes academic misconduct. Resubmitting work previously submitted for credit in another course without prior written instructor approval is also prohibited. Assignments submitted through the course management system are subject to plagiarism detection review. Students must retain copies of all submitted work and drafts as proof of authorship. In cases where authorship is questioned, students may be required to defend their work orally or provide evidence of the development process.

---

**Chunk ID:** AI-010
**Title:** AI Tool Usage — General Policy
**Content:** The use of Artificial Intelligence (AI) tools in academic work at AAI is subject to specific policies established at the course and program level. The default position is that AI-generated content submitted as original student work without disclosure and instructor authorization constitutes academic misconduct. Each course syllabus will specify the permitted level of AI tool use, ranging from full prohibition to permitted use with mandatory disclosure. Students are responsible for understanding and following the AI use policy in each course they are enrolled in. Questions about whether a specific tool or use case is permitted should be directed to the course instructor before submitting work. Uncertainty about AI policies does not excuse non-compliance.

---

**Chunk ID:** AI-011
**Title:** Generative AI — Disclosure Requirements
**Content:** When a course instructor at AAI explicitly permits the use of generative AI tools in academic work, students must disclose their use transparently in all submissions where AI assistance was employed. The disclosure must include: the name of the AI tool used, the nature of the AI assistance (e.g., brainstorming, drafting, editing, coding), and a statement that the student has reviewed, verified, and taken responsibility for the final content. A standardized AI Use Disclosure Statement template is available on the AAI Learning Management System (LMS). Disclosure must be included in the submission itself, not only in a cover communication. Failing to disclose AI use in a course that permits but requires disclosure is treated as a form of academic dishonesty.

---

**Chunk ID:** AI-012
**Title:** Generative AI — Prohibited Use Cases
**Content:** Regardless of course-level permissions, certain uses of generative AI are prohibited at AAI under all circumstances. Prohibited AI use cases include: submitting AI-generated text as entirely one's own work without disclosure; using AI to complete examinations or proctored assessments; using AI to fabricate research data, citations, or experimental results; using AI to produce work for another student; and using AI to circumvent accessibility accommodations review. AI tools that access live data or external networks during closed-book assessments are also prohibited. These prohibitions apply even if a student believes AI use would not be detected. The institution reserves the right to update the list of prohibited use cases as technology evolves, with notice given to students.

---

**Chunk ID:** AI-013
**Title:** Responsible AI Use — Ethical Considerations
**Content:** AAI encourages students to engage with AI tools critically, ethically, and thoughtfully. Responsible AI use means understanding the limitations and potential biases of AI systems, not treating AI-generated content as authoritative without verification, being transparent about AI involvement, and maintaining one's own intellectual development. Students are encouraged to use AI as a learning aid rather than as a replacement for their own thinking. Over-reliance on AI tools may impede the development of critical thinking, writing, research, and problem-solving skills that are central to a quality education. AAI's academic programs are designed to help students develop these skills, and circumventing that process through excessive AI reliance is contrary to the educational mission.

**Chunk ID:** AI-014
**Title:** Academic Misconduct Investigations — Process Overview
**Content:** When a potential academic integrity violation is identified at AAI, the faculty member or staff member who discovers it must report it to the Office of Academic Integrity (OAI) within 5 business days using the Misconduct Report Form. The OAI acknowledges receipt and assigns a case officer within 3 business days. The student is notified in writing of the alleged violation, the evidence on record, and their rights in the process. The student has 10 business days to submit a written response. The case officer reviews all evidence and the student's response and issues a preliminary finding. If the finding is that a violation occurred, a hearing may be scheduled depending on the severity of the case. Throughout the process, the student is presumed to be acting in good faith until the investigation is complete.

---

**Chunk ID:** AI-015
**Title:** Academic Misconduct — Student Rights During Investigation
**Content:** Students accused of academic misconduct at AAI have the right to: receive written notice of the specific allegation and supporting evidence; submit a written statement in response to the allegation within 10 business days; appear before the Academic Integrity Review Panel if a formal hearing is held; present evidence and witnesses on their behalf; be accompanied by a student advocate (a currently enrolled AAI student in good standing) during formal hearings; and receive a written decision with rationale. Students are not represented by legal counsel during internal proceedings, but may seek personal legal advice outside the process. The rights described in this policy apply regardless of the student's year of study, program, or prior academic record.

---

**Chunk ID:** AI-016
**Title:** Academic Misconduct — Hearing Procedures
**Content:** When an academic misconduct case at AAI proceeds to a formal hearing, the Academic Integrity Review Panel convenes within 20 business days of the preliminary finding. The panel is composed of two faculty members, one academic administrator, and one student representative (from outside the respondent's program). The hearing is not a legal proceeding; formal rules of evidence do not apply. Both the reporting party and the student respondent may present statements and evidence. The panel deliberates privately and reaches a decision by majority vote. The decision and rationale are communicated to the student in writing within 5 business days of the hearing. Panel members with a direct interest in the case must recuse themselves.

---

**Chunk ID:** AI-017
**Title:** Academic Misconduct — Sanctions and Penalties
**Content:** Sanctions for academic misconduct at AAI are determined based on the severity of the violation, whether it is a first or repeat offense, and mitigating or aggravating circumstances. Possible sanctions include: a written warning; requirement to complete an academic integrity education module; grade reduction or zero on the affected assignment; failing grade in the course; notation of academic misconduct on the student's internal record; suspension for one or more semesters; or permanent dismissal from AAI. Sanctions are applied progressively for repeat violations. Suspension and dismissal are the most serious sanctions and require approval from the Dean of the relevant college. All sanctions are communicated in writing with explanation of the rationale.

---

**Chunk ID:** AI-018
**Title:** Academic Misconduct Appeals — Process
**Content:** Students who wish to appeal a finding or sanction in an academic misconduct case at AAI must submit an appeal to the Academic Appeals Committee within 15 business days of receiving the written decision. Appeals must be based on: a procedural error that materially affected the outcome; new evidence not available at the time of the original hearing; or a disproportionate sanction relative to the nature of the offense. Appeals are reviewed by a subcommittee of the Academic Appeals Committee that did not participate in the original hearing. The subcommittee may uphold, modify, or overturn the original decision. The subcommittee's decision is communicated within 20 business days and is final. The committee may not increase the severity of the sanction upon appeal.

**Chunk ID:** AI-019
**Title:** Plagiarism Detection — Institutional Use of Technology
**Content:** AAI utilizes plagiarism detection software integrated with the Learning Management System (LMS) to review academic submissions for text similarity. All written assignments are submitted through the LMS and screened automatically. Similarity reports are reviewed by instructors and do not, on their own, constitute evidence of plagiarism. A high similarity score triggers instructor review; context, citation practices, and the nature of the assignment are considered before any determination of misconduct is made. Students may review their own similarity reports before final submission where the system allows it. Use of plagiarism detection is not a substitute for faculty judgment and is one tool among many used to promote academic integrity. Students are not penalized solely on the basis of a similarity score.

**Chunk ID:** AI-020
**Title:** Contract Cheating — Policy
**Content:** Contract cheating, defined as the act of commissioning, purchasing, or otherwise obtaining academic work from a third party (human or AI) for submission as one's own, is strictly prohibited at AAI and constitutes a severe form of academic misconduct. This includes the use of essay mills, freelance academic writing services, or any commercial or peer arrangement where another party completes assessed work on the student's behalf. Online platforms offering academic completion services are included in this prohibition. Contract cheating is treated as a first-degree violation with a presumptive outcome of a failing course grade and possible suspension or dismissal. Detection methods include stylometric analysis, oral defenses, and submission metadata review.

---

**Chunk ID:** AI-021
**Title:** Self-Plagiarism — Policy
**Content:** Self-plagiarism at AAI occurs when a student resubmits previously graded work, in whole or in part, for a new assessment without explicit written permission from the instructor of the current course. Students may not recycle essays, reports, or other previously submitted work even if that work was produced by the student alone. If a student wishes to build on prior work, they must disclose the prior submission, obtain written instructor approval, and clearly differentiate new contributions from previously submitted content. Reusing one's own work without disclosure is considered dishonest because it misrepresents the originality of the current submission. Policies on self-plagiarism apply equally to work submitted in previous semesters, including work submitted at other institutions.

**Title:** Data Ethics — Student Research Responsibilities
**Content:** Students at AAI who collect, process, or analyze data as part of their academic work are expected to adhere to rigorous data ethics standards. This includes: obtaining proper consent before collecting data from human participants; storing data securely and confidentially; not sharing identifiable participant data without authorization; accurately reporting data without selective omission or manipulation; and properly disposing of sensitive data after its academic purpose is fulfilled. Students must acknowledge all data sources used in their work. Using publicly available datasets still requires proper citation. Misrepresenting data sources, using data without authorization, or failing to protect participant confidentiality constitutes academic misconduct and may also violate applicable data protection regulations.

**Chunk ID:** AI-026
**Title:** Ghostwriting — Policy
**Content:** Ghostwriting in an academic context—defined as having another person write, substantially revise, or generate content on behalf of a student for submission as the student's own work—is prohibited at AAI. This applies to human ghostwriters as well as AI-generated text submitted as personally authored work. It includes arrangements with tutors, peers, or paid services that cross the boundary from legitimate editing assistance into substantial content generation. The boundary between permissible editing (grammar, clarity, formatting) and prohibited ghostwriting (content creation, argument development, substantive rewrites) is that permissible editing does not change the student's original ideas or voice. When in doubt, students should consult with their instructor before seeking external editing assistance.

**Chunk ID:** AI-027
**Title:** Group Project Integrity — Policy
**Content:** Academic integrity obligations apply equally to group projects at AAI. Each member of a group is responsible for the integrity of the entire submitted work. If any portion of a group submission contains plagiarized, fabricated, or dishonestly produced content, all group members may be held responsible unless a member can demonstrate that they had no knowledge of and did not contribute to the misconduct. Groups are encouraged to maintain transparency in the division of labor and to document contributions. Instructors may require individual reflection components or oral presentations to verify individual understanding of group work. Students who are aware that a group member is engaging in misconduct are expected to report it or address it within the group; remaining silent when aware of misconduct may be considered complicity.

**Chunk ID:** AI-028
**Title:** Impersonation — Policy
**Content:** Impersonation in an academic context at AAI refers to taking an examination, completing an online assessment, or submitting academic work on behalf of another student, or having another person do so on one's behalf. This includes both in-person and online impersonation. Impersonation is one of the most serious forms of academic misconduct and may result in immediate suspension or dismissal for all parties involved. Identity verification measures are in place for online assessments, including proctoring systems and authentication requirements. Students must verify their own identity at the commencement of proctored examinations. Attempting to deceive identity verification systems constitutes impersonation. Both the impersonator and the student on whose behalf they act are subject to penalties.

Faculty members may not unilaterally impose grade penalties as a substitute for formal reporting unless the case involves a minor first offense as defined in the Policy on Minor Infractions (see AI-043). Failure to report suspected misconduct is a policy violation. Faculty should preserve all relevant evidence (submission files, timestamps, similarity reports, exam materials) and maintain confidentiality until the investigation is concluded. Faculty members may not retaliate against students they have reported or treat suspected students differently in grading while a case is pending.

**Chunk ID:** AI-043
**Title:** Minor Infractions — Informal Resolution Process
**Content:** For first-time, minor academic integrity violations at AAI—such as minor citation errors that do not constitute deliberate plagiarism, inadvertent failure to disclose minor AI assistance, or unintentional unauthorized collaboration on low-stakes assignments—instructors may choose an informal resolution process rather than a formal referral to the OAI. Informal resolution options include requiring the student to resubmit corrected work, issuing a written warning, or assigning a reduced grade on the affected assessment, with the student's written acknowledgment of the policy violation. Informal resolutions must be documented and reported to the OAI within 10 business days, even though no formal penalty is imposed. If the same student commits a second offense, the informal resolution record is used to establish the prior history.

**Chunk ID:** AI-044
**Title:** AI-Generated Images and Media — Policy
**Content:** The use of AI tools to generate images, audio, video, or other media for academic submissions at AAI is governed by the same principles as AI text generation. AI-generated media must be disclosed in submissions when used, consistent with the course-level AI Use Policy. AI-generated media that is used to misrepresent reality—such as fabricated photographs presented as genuine documentary evidence, or AI voices passed off as real interview recordings—constitutes fabrication and is academic misconduct regardless of course permissions. Creative courses that incorporate AI-generated media must follow instructor-specified disclosure requirements. AI-generated media used in research, journalism, or documentary projects must be clearly labeled as AI-generated in all presentations and publications.

**Chunk ID:** AI-045
**Title:** Collusion — Definition and Policy
**Content:** Collusion at AAI is defined as a secret or informal agreement between two or more students to misrepresent the authorship or originality of submitted academic work. This is distinct from permitted group work and from general academic discussions. Collusion occurs when students agree to present jointly produced work as individually authored, share answers to individual assessments in advance, or coordinate in other ways designed to deceive instructors about the nature of submitted work. Both colluding parties are subject to the same penalties as if each had independently committed the underlying violation. Collusion is often identified through stylometric analysis, submission timing patterns, and cross-comparison of student submissions. Students involved in collusion arrangements should cease immediately and may self-report to the OAI with reduced penalty consideration.

**Chunk ID:** AI-046
**Title:** Academic Integrity Training — Faculty Requirements
**Content:** Faculty members at AAI are required to complete academic integrity training during their onboarding period and to participate in a refresher session at least once every three academic years. Training covers the Academic Integrity Policy, reporting procedures, proper syllabus AI policy statements, designing assessments that discourage misconduct, and supporting students in developing integrity-consistent practices. Instructors are encouraged to design assessments with multiple drafts, personalized prompts, and oral components to reduce opportunities for contract cheating and AI misuse. Faculty who design entirely new assessment formats are encouraged to consult with the Office of Academic Integrity for review before administering them. Assessment design guidance is available through the Center for Teaching and Learning.

---

**Chunk ID:** AI-047
**Title:** Integrity in Laboratory and Field Work — Policy
**Content:** Students conducting laboratory or field-based academic work at AAI are subject to integrity standards specific to empirical investigation. Data must be collected and recorded honestly, without fabrication, falsification, or selective manipulation. Students may not present another student's lab results as their own. Lab reports must reflect the actual procedures followed, including any deviations from the prescribed protocol, which must be noted honestly. Data that does not support the expected hypothesis must be reported accurately; suppression of negative or unexpected results is a form of fabrication. Lab notebooks and field notes may be reviewed as evidence of original data collection. Shared lab data in group experiments must be attributed appropriately in individual reports.

---

**Chunk ID:** AI-048
**Title:** Integrity During Take-Home Assessments — Policy
**Content:** Take-home examinations and assessments at AAI are subject to the Academic Integrity Policy in the same way as in-person assessments. Unless the instructor explicitly permits open collaboration, take-home assessments are to be completed independently. Students may use course materials and approved resources but may not seek assistance from other individuals, online tutoring services, or AI tools beyond what is explicitly authorized in the instructions. Submitting take-home work that was produced collaboratively when not authorized is unauthorized collaboration. The fact that a take-home assessment is unproctored does not reduce the student's integrity obligations. Instructors may design take-home assessments with personalized prompts, unique data sets, or required oral defenses to verify independent completion.

---

**Chunk ID:** AI-049
**Title:** Confidentiality During Misconduct Proceedings
**Content:** All parties involved in academic misconduct proceedings at AAI are required to maintain confidentiality regarding the details of the case. Students, faculty, and staff who participate in a misconduct investigation or hearing must not disclose information about the case to individuals who are not part of the proceedings. The student respondent may share information with their student advocate or personal legal advisor. Breach of confidentiality during active proceedings may be treated as interference with the investigation and may constitute a Student Code of Conduct violation. Case outcomes are communicated only to the parties directly involved, including the reporting faculty member, the student respondent, and relevant academic administrators. Media inquiries related to ongoing cases are directed to the Office of Communications.

**Chunk ID:** AI-050
**Title:** Annual Academic Integrity Report — Transparency Policy
**Content:** The Office of Academic Integrity at AAI publishes an Annual Academic Integrity Report at the end of each academic year. The report provides aggregate, anonymized data on the number of misconduct cases reported, the types of violations identified, the outcomes of investigations, and trends across academic departments. The report is intended to promote institutional transparency and to support evidence-based improvements to academic integrity education and policy. Individual case details are not disclosed in the report. The report is available to students, faculty, and staff through the OAI website. Findings from the report are used by the Academic Affairs Committee to review and update policies, assessment design guidelines, and integrity training programs.

**Content:** Final examinations at AAI are administered during a dedicated Final Examination Period at the end of each fall and spring semester. **Chunk ID:** EA-003

**Content:** A makeup examination at AAI may be granted when a student is unable to attend a scheduled exam due to a documented, legitimate reason. **Chunk ID:** EA-004
**Title:** Late Submission Policy — Assignments
**Content:** The default late submission policy at AAI applies when an instructor has not specified an alternative policy in the course syllabus. Assignments submitted more than 5 calendar days after the deadline will not be accepted and will receive a grade of zero, unless the student has been granted a formal extension. Instructors may establish alternative late policies in their syllabus, which take precedence over the default. Students must request extensions before the deadline; retroactive extension requests are not accepted except in cases of documented emergency verified by the instructor.

**Content:** Students who anticipate they will be unable to meet an assignment deadline due to documented extenuating circumstances may request an extension from their instructor. Instructors have discretion to approve or deny extension requests and should communicate their decision within 24 hours of receiving a complete request. Washroom breaks are permitted during the main examination period with proctor approval, one student at a time.

**Chunk ID:** EA-009
**Title:** Remote Proctoring — Policy and Notification
**Content:** For select online assessments, AAI uses remote proctoring technology to maintain examination integrity. **Chunk ID:** EA-010
**Title:** Grade Review — Student Rights
**Content:** Students at AAI who believe their final course grade has been assigned in error may initiate a formal Grade Review. The first step is an informal review, in which the student contacts the instructor within 10 business days of the final grade posting to request an explanation and review of grading. If the informal review does not resolve the concern, the student may submit a formal Grade Review petition to the department chair within 15 business days of the informal review response. The petition must specify the basis for the review, including the claimed error in calculation or application of grading criteria. Department chairs review petitions and issue a decision within 20 business days. Grade changes may be approved only on the grounds of computational error, misapplication of grading criteria, or procedural irregularity.

**Chunk ID:** EA-011

**Content:** Students with documented accommodations through the Office of Accessibility Services (OAS) who require examination modifications (extended time, separate room, reader, scribe, etc.) must arrange accommodated testing through the OAS Testing Center or directly with their instructor, depending on the accommodation type. If accommodated testing is arranged directly with the instructor, the Accommodation Letter must have been delivered beforehand.

**Chunk ID:** EA-012
**Title:** Examination Conduct — Proctor Authority
**Content:** Proctors administering examinations at AAI have full authority to maintain order, enforce examination rules, and address integrity concerns during the examination period. Disputes regarding proctor decisions during an examination are addressed after the examination through the Office of Academic Affairs; they cannot be appealed in real-time during the exam. A proctor's observation of suspected misconduct during an examination is submitted in writing to the Office of Academic Integrity as an official report. Students who behave in a threatening or abusive manner toward proctors are subject to immediate removal and a conduct referral.

**Chunk ID:** EA-013

**Content:** Oral examinations may be used as assessment tools at AAI at the instructor's discretion, provided they are announced in the course syllabus or at least 10 calendar days before administration. **Chunk ID:** EA-014

**Chunk ID:** EA-016
**Title:** Grading Scale and GPA Equivalencies
**Content:** The official AAI grading scale for undergraduate coursework assigns the following grade points: A (4.0), A- (3.7), B+ (3.3), B (3.0), B- (2.7), C+ (2.3), C (2.0), C- (1.7), D+ (1.3), D (1.0), F (0.0). Grades of W, I, P/F, AU, and S/U do not carry quality point equivalents and are excluded from GPA calculations. The grading scale is consistent institution-wide and may not be modified by individual instructors or departments. Students should refer to their program handbook for minimum grade requirements within their specific degree program.

**Chunk ID:** EA-017
**Title:** Assessment Feedback — Instructor Obligations
**Content:** Instructors at AAI are required to provide substantive, actionable feedback on all major assessed work (assignments worth 15% or more of the final course grade) within 14 calendar days of the submission deadline. For examinations, instructors must post a general answer key or grade summary to the LMS within 7 calendar days of the exam. Students who do not receive feedback within the specified timeframe should contact their instructor; repeated non-compliance may be reported to the department chair. Feedback is integral to the learning process and must not be withheld.

**Chunk ID:** EA-018

**Content:** Continuous assessment components at AAI—including reading quizzes, weekly reflections, participation logs, and in-class exercises—are designed to support regular student engagement with course material. Makeup opportunities for missed continuous assessment activities are at the instructor's discretion and are not guaranteed. **Chunk ID:** EA-019

**Chunk ID:** EA-020
**Title:** Academic Dishonesty During Assessment — Immediate Consequences
**Content:** When a proctor or instructor witnesses or has sufficient reason to suspect academic dishonesty during an examination at AAI, they may collect the student's exam materials and note the time of the incident without publicly confronting the student. **Content:** Many undergraduate and all graduate programs at AAI require the completion of a Capstone Project or Thesis as a summative program-level assessment. **Chunk ID:** EA-022
**Title:** Grading Appeals — Time Limits
**Content:** Students at AAI who wish to appeal a grade must act within strict time limits to ensure their concerns are addressed. No grade appeal will be considered if submitted more than one full semester after the grade was posted. Appeals submitted outside these deadlines are not accepted, regardless of the stated grounds. Students who are unaware of their grade due to a posting error should document the issue and contact the Registrar's Office promptly to preserve their appeal rights.

**Chunk ID:** EA-023
**Title:** Formative vs. Summative Assessment — Definitions
**Content:** AAI distinguishes between formative and summative assessment in its instructional policy. Formative assessments are low-stakes, ongoing activities (e.g., quizzes, drafts, participation, peer reviews) designed to provide feedback and support learning in progress. They typically constitute 20% or less of the final grade. Summative assessments are higher-stakes evaluations (e.g., final exams, major research papers, presentations, portfolios) designed to measure the extent of student learning at the end of a unit, semester, or program. Both assessment types are essential components of good course design. Instructors are encouraged to balance formative and summative assessments to support diverse learning needs. Policies on extensions, makeups, and late submissions may differ for formative and summative assessments.

**Chunk ID:** EA-024
**Title:** Anonymous Grading — Instructor Option
**Content:** To minimize unconscious bias in grading, instructors at AAI are encouraged, though not required, to use anonymous grading for major written assessments. When anonymous grading is used, students should not include their names on submitted documents, only their student identification number. Instructors who choose to use anonymous grading must notify students in the syllabus. Anonymous grading is not applicable for presentations, group projects, oral examinations, laboratory assessments, or other assessments where student identity is inherent to the activity. Adoption of anonymous grading practices is tracked as part of the institution's commitment to equitable assessment.

**Content:** Students at AAI who have two or more final examinations scheduled at the same time, or three or more final examinations in a single calendar day, are entitled to schedule conflict resolution. **Chunk ID:** EA-026

**Chunk ID:** EA-027
**Title:** Thesis and Dissertation Defense — Assessment Policy
**Content:** Graduate students completing a thesis or dissertation at AAI must successfully complete a formal oral defense before a supervisory committee. The defense is a public academic event and must be announced at least 10 business days in advance. The committee, composed of at least three faculty members including the primary supervisor, evaluates the written thesis and the student's oral defense. Possible outcomes include: Pass without revisions; Pass with minor revisions (revisions due within 30 days); Pass with major revisions (revisions due within 90 days and subject to committee re-review); or Fail (student may re-defend once within 12 months). Plagiarism or fabrication in the thesis or dissertation results in immediate academic misconduct proceedings and may result in withdrawal of any degree awarded.

**Chunk ID:** RG-001
**Title:** Registration Process — Step-by-Step Overview
**Content:** Course registration at AAI follows a defined process conducted through the Student Academic Portal (SAP). Step 2: Students search for courses using the Course Catalog search function by subject, course number, or instructor. Step 3: Students review course details including meeting times, credit hours, prerequisites, and seat availability. Step 4: Students add desired courses to their shopping cart and verify there are no time conflicts or prerequisite issues. Any discrepancies should be reported to the Registrar's Office immediately. Registration is not confirmed until the student receives the confirmation email.

**Chunk ID:** RG-002
**Title:** Registration Holds — Types and Resolution
**Content:** A registration hold at AAI prevents a student from accessing the course registration system until the hold is resolved. Common types of holds include: Financial Hold (outstanding balance with the Office of Financial Services); Academic Hold (placed by the Office of Academic Affairs for issues such as mandatory advising non-compliance); Administrative Hold (placed by a specific department for outstanding requirements); and Immunization Hold (incomplete health documentation required by Student Health Services). Students can view active holds on their SAP dashboard. Each hold type displays the office responsible for resolution. Students must resolve holds before their registration window opens to register without interruption. Holds do not affect current enrollment but do prevent new registrations.

**Chunk ID:** RG-003
**Title:** Waitlist Policy — Course Enrollment
**Content:** When a course reaches its maximum enrollment capacity at AAI, qualified students may join the course waitlist through the SAP. Waitlist positions are assigned on a first-come, first-served basis. If an enrolled student drops the course during the Add/Drop period, the next student on the waitlist is automatically notified by email and given 24 hours to confirm enrollment through the SAP. Students who do not confirm within 24 hours lose their waitlist position. Students on a waitlist are not officially enrolled and may not attend class unless the instructor grants written permission for provisional attendance. Waitlist status does not guarantee enrollment. Students on a waitlist should maintain alternative course options in case a seat does not become available.

**Chunk ID:** RG-004
**Title:** Prerequisites — Verification at Registration
**Content:** The AAI Student Academic Portal (SAP) automatically checks prerequisite requirements when a student attempts to register for a course. If the system detects that the student has not satisfied a listed prerequisite, registration is blocked and an error message is displayed. Students who believe they have equivalent preparation (e.g., via transfer credits, AP credits, or professional experience) and wish to request a prerequisite waiver must contact the relevant academic department. Waiver requests are processed manually and may take up to 5 business days. Students should not assume a waiver will be granted and should maintain alternative registration options. Manual overrides entered by department staff are logged in the student's academic file. Prerequisite waivers are course-specific and do not carry over to other courses.

**Chunk ID:** RG-005
**Title:** Course Section Changes — Policy
**Content:** Students who wish to change their enrollment from one section of a course to another (e.g., from a Monday/Wednesday section to a Tuesday/Thursday section of the same course) may do so during the Add/Drop period through the SAP, subject to seat availability. Section changes are not guaranteed and depend on space availability and administrative feasibility. Students who change sections are responsible for any course content or assignments they may have missed in the new section. Grading continuity between sections is the student's responsibility, not the institution's.

**Content:** The Graduation Audit at AAI is an official review conducted by the Registrar's Office upon receipt of a student's Graduation Application. Students are strongly encouraged to conduct an informal degree audit with their academic advisor at least one semester before submitting the formal Graduation Application.

Provisional clearance may be issued for students completing minor outstanding requirements, at the discretion of the Dean of the relevant college.

Students are responsible for meeting all graduation application deadlines; reminders are posted through the SAP and institutional email.

To request a makeup exam, submit a written request with supporting documentation (e.g., medical certificate, official notice) within 3 business days of the missed exam. **Title:** FAQ — What is the Add/Drop deadline and what does it mean?
**Content:** **Q: What is the Add/Drop deadline and what happens if I miss it?**
A: The Add/Drop period lasts for the first 10 calendar days of each semester. During this time, you can add open courses, drop courses without any grade penalty, and switch sections without a fee. Courses dropped during this period do not appear on your transcript. After the Add/Drop deadline, you cannot add new courses. If you need to leave a course after this deadline, you must submit a formal Course Withdrawal, which results in a "W" grade on your transcript. Withdrawals are permitted up to the end of Week 10; later withdrawals require Dean's approval for extenuating circumstances only. (See AP-005, AP-006.)

**Chunk ID:** FAQ-004
**Title:** FAQ — How is my GPA calculated?
**Content:** **Q: How is my GPA calculated?**
A: Your cumulative GPA is calculated by dividing your total quality points by your total attempted credit hours. Quality points are the product of your grade point value and the credit hours for each course. The AAI grade scale is: A = 4.0, A- = 3.7, B+ = 3.3, B = 3.0, B- = 2.7, C+ = 2.3, C = 2.0, C- = 1.7, D+ = 1.3, D = 1.0, F = 0.0. Grades of W (withdrawal), I (incomplete), P (pass), and AU (audit) are not included in GPA calculations. Transfer credit grades are also excluded from your AAI cumulative GPA. (See AP-007, EA-016.)

**Chunk ID:** FAQ-005
**Title:** FAQ — What does Academic Probation mean and how do I get off it?
**Content:** **Q: I received a notice saying I'm on Academic Probation. What does this mean?**
A: Academic Probation is placed when your cumulative GPA falls below 2.0 or your completion rate drops below 67% of attempted credit hours. While on probation, you are limited to 13 credit hours per semester and must meet with your academic advisor at least every four weeks. You must also complete an Academic Success Plan (ASP). Probation is reviewed at the end of the following semester. If your GPA rises to 2.0 or above and your completion rate improves to 67% or higher, you will be returned to Good Standing. If you remain below 2.0, you may face Academic Suspension. (See AP-009, AP-010.)

**Title:** FAQ — Can I use AI tools for my assignments?
**Content:** **Q: Am I allowed to use AI tools like chatbots for my coursework?**
A: It depends on your specific course. Each instructor is required to include an AI Use Policy in the course syllabus. There are three possible levels: (1) AI is fully prohibited for all assessed work; (2) AI is permitted for specific tasks only, with mandatory disclosure; or (3) AI is permitted for all non-proctored work with mandatory disclosure. When in doubt, ask your instructor in writing before using any AI tool for assessed work. (See AI-010, AI-011, AI-025.)

**Chunk ID:** FAQ-015
**Title:** FAQ — What is the difference between Probation and Suspension?
**Content:** **Q: What is the difference between Academic Probation and Academic Suspension?**
A: Academic Probation is a warning status that occurs when your cumulative GPA falls below 2.0 or your completion rate drops below 67%. While on probation, you may continue to enroll (limited to 13 credit hours) and have the opportunity to improve. Academic Suspension is more serious: it occurs when you fail to recover from probation or your GPA falls to 1.0 or below at any point. Suspension requires you to sit out for one full semester. After suspension, you must apply for reinstatement through the Office of Academic Affairs. If you are suspended a second time, this may result in Academic Dismissal. (See AP-009, AP-010, AP-011.)

**Chunk ID:** FAQ-016
**Title:** FAQ — Can I retake a course to improve my GPA?
**Content:** **Q: Can I repeat a course to improve my GPA?**
A: Yes. You may repeat any course in which you received a grade of C- or below, including F. When you repeat a course, only the most recent grade is used in your cumulative GPA calculation, though both attempts remain visible on your transcript with an "R" notation on the original. You may repeat a course a maximum of two times (three total attempts). A third repeat requires written approval from the Dean of your college. Note that repeating a course does not retroactively change your GPA from prior semesters or affect prior academic standing decisions, Dean's List designations, or financial aid calculations already applied. (See AP-030.)

**Chunk ID:** FAQ-019
**Title:** FAQ — I'm an international student — do I have special enrollment requirements?
**Content:** **Q: Are there special enrollment requirements I must follow as an international student?**
A: Yes. International students on standard student visas must maintain full-time enrollment of at least 12 credit hours per semester. Dropping below this threshold without an official reduced course load authorization from the International Student Services Office (ISSO) may jeopardize your visa status. A reduced course load may only be authorized for documented medical conditions, specific academic circumstances in your final semester, or initial program enrollment. Before making any enrollment changes — including dropping a course or taking a medical leave — contact ISSO. ISSO advisors coordinate closely with academic advisors to ensure your enrollment decisions comply with visa requirements. (See SS-011, SS-012.)

**Content:** **Q: I think my final grade was calculated incorrectly. What can I do?**
A: Start by contacting your instructor informally within 10 business days of the final grade posting to request an explanation and review.
